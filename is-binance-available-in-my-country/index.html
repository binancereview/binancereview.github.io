Is Binance Available in My Country?

Binance's availability is not uniform worldwide, and it has changed more than usual in 2026. This page summarizes the current situation as of this writing, but exchange availability can shift with little notice, so always confirm on Binance's own status page before assuming access.

Last checked: July 2026. Regulatory status changes quickly in this industry; treat the information below as a starting point, not a final answer.

United States

US residents cannot use the main Binance.com platform. US IP addresses are geo-blocked, and residency is checked during identity verification. The only legal entry point for US customers is Binance.US, a separate company with a substantially smaller product set: spot trading only, no futures, no margin, no options, and a shorter list of supported coins. Binance.US does not operate in every US state.

European Union

The EU's MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets) regulation requires any exchange serving EU customers to hold a license from at least one member state. Binance missed the transitional licensing deadline of July 1, 2026, and as a result suspended new spot and margin trading for users in France and several other EU countries, including Italy, Poland, and Spain. Affected users can still withdraw existing funds but cannot place new trades until Binance secures the required license. Binance has said it intends to return once licensed, without a confirmed timeline. If you're in an affected EU country, check Binance's status page directly before signing up expecting full access.

United Kingdom

The UK sits outside the EU's MiCA framework and has its own regulatory regime through the FCA, which has periodically restricted specific product categories (particularly derivatives marketed to retail users) rather than blocking the exchange outright. Product availability for UK users should be checked against current FCA-related restrictions rather than assumed.

Rest of the world

Outside the US and the EU countries currently affected by the MiCA suspension, Binance operates in most countries without the restrictions described above, though local rules on specific products (like futures or margin) still vary. Latin America, most of Asia, the Middle East, and Africa are generally served by the standard Binance.com platform, subject to each country's own local regulation.

Frequently asked questions

Why did Binance suspend trading in parts of the EU?

Binance did not secure a MiCA license in an EU member state before the July 1, 2026 transitional deadline, which under EU law requires suspending crypto trading services for customers in affected countries until a license is obtained.

Can I still withdraw funds if my country is affected?

Yes, according to Binance's own communications, withdrawals remain available for affected EU users even though new trading is paused.

Is Binance available in Latin America?

Generally yes, through the standard Binance.com platform, though individual countries may have their own local rules on specific products.
