How to Buy Bitcoin on Binance

Buying Bitcoin on Binance takes five steps: create an account, verify your identity, fund the account, place a spot buy order, and store or hold your Bitcoin. This guide covers each step at a general level; the exact screens can vary slightly by country and by app version, so treat this as a roadmap rather than a pixel-by-pixel walkthrough.

Step 1: Create an account

Sign up with an email address or phone number and set a password. Binance will send a verification code to confirm the account is really yours before it becomes active.

Step 2: Complete identity verification

Before you can deposit or trade, you'll need to complete identity verification (KYC), typically a government-issued ID plus a selfie for confirmation. This step is required by regulation in essentially every country Binance operates in, and it's what allows the account to move real money.

Step 3: Fund your account

Deposit methods vary by country. Common options include bank transfer, card payment, or a local instant payment method where available. Card payments are usually the fastest but carry the highest fees; bank transfer is typically the cheapest but slower.

Step 4: Buy Bitcoin

With funds available, go to the spot trading or "buy crypto" section, select Bitcoin (BTC), and enter either the amount of Bitcoin you want or the amount of money you want to spend. Binance will calculate the other side automatically at the current market rate. Review the fee shown before confirming.

Step 5: Decide where to hold it

You can leave your Bitcoin in your Binance account for easy access to trading, or withdraw it to a separate wallet you control for longer-term storage. Many experienced users treat funds actively used for trading and funds meant to be held long-term as two different things, moving the latter off the exchange.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to buy a whole Bitcoin?

No. Bitcoin can be purchased in fractions, so you can buy any amount that fits your budget, down to a very small fraction of one coin.

How long does identity verification take?

It's often completed within minutes, though it can take longer depending on document quality and verification volume at the time.

What's the cheapest way to fund my account?

Bank transfer is typically the cheapest deposit method, though it may be slower than a card payment. Check the fee shown for each method before choosing.
