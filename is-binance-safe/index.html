Is Binance Safe? A Balanced Review

Short answer: Binance is the largest and most liquid crypto exchange in the world, with real security infrastructure and a public reserve fund, but it also carries a documented history of regulatory penalties and ongoing compliance scrutiny. Whether it's "safe" depends on what you're using it for and where you live. Here's the full picture.

What Binance does well

Regulatory history: what actually happened

In November 2023, Binance and founder Changpeng Zhao pleaded guilty to violating US anti-money-laundering laws. The company paid a combined settlement of roughly $4.3 billion to the Department of Justice, FinCEN, OFAC, and the CFTC, and agreed to operate under an independent compliance monitor for five years with access to its books and transaction data. Zhao stepped down as CEO and later served a US prison sentence. Richard Teng, a former regulator at Singapore's Monetary Authority and the Abu Dhabi Global Market, became CEO and has publicly prioritized compliance since taking over.

In February 2026, Fortune reported that Binance had dismissed several compliance employees who had flagged over a billion dollars in transactions allegedly linked to Iranian entities. Binance and Teng publicly disputed the characterization of the report. As of this writing, no regulatory action has been announced in connection with these specific allegations, and the situation should be treated as unresolved rather than settled either way.

Regional availability: it varies, and it changes

Binance's product availability is not the same everywhere, and it has changed significantly in 2026. US residents cannot use the main Binance.com platform at all; they are directed to Binance.US, a separate entity with a much smaller product set limited to spot trading, no futures, no margin. In the European Union, new licensing rules under MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets) took effect, and Binance missed the licensing deadline in several member states, resulting in a suspension of trading services in France and a number of other EU countries starting July 2026. Affected users can still withdraw funds but cannot place new trades until Binance secures the required license. Given how often this changes, always check Binance's own status page for your specific country before assuming a feature is available.

Reserves and insurance fund

Binance publishes a quarterly Proof-of-Reserves report using a Merkle tree structure, which allows individual users to verify that their balance was included in the published snapshot. It also maintains SAFU (Secure Asset Fund for Users), an insurance reserve funded by a portion of trading fees since 2018. SAFU has been used publicly at least twice: once after a 2019 security breach, and once to cover users affected by a 2022 bridge incident on BNB Chain. These mechanisms add a layer of transparency that not every exchange offers, though they are not a guarantee against every possible risk.

Customer support and user complaints

Independent review platforms show a pattern of complaints about slow or unhelpful customer support, particularly around account verification and withdrawal issues. This is worth weighing seriously if you expect to need quick human help, and it's one of the more consistent criticisms across large exchanges generally, not unique to Binance, but Binance's scale means the volume of complaints is correspondingly large.

Bottom line

Binance's core infrastructure, liquidity, and reserve transparency are genuinely strong. Its regulatory history is real and shouldn't be waved away, but it's also several years old and came with a settlement, an independent monitor, and a leadership change specifically aimed at fixing the underlying compliance problems. Whether that's an acceptable trade-off for you depends on your own risk tolerance and where you live, since availability and product access can differ by country and can change without much notice.

Frequently asked questions

Has Binance ever lost customer funds?

Binance disclosed a security breach in 2019 that resulted in a loss of Bitcoin, which was covered by its SAFU insurance fund rather than affecting user balances directly. No similar large-scale loss of customer funds has been publicly reported since.

Can I use Binance in the United States?

Not on the main Binance.com platform. US residents are required to use Binance.US, a separate entity with a smaller set of available products.

Is Binance available in the European Union?

Availability varies by country and has changed recently due to MiCA licensing requirements. Some EU countries had trading suspended in mid-2026 after Binance missed a licensing deadline. Always check the current status for your specific country before signing up.

What is the SAFU fund?

SAFU (Secure Asset Fund for Users) is an insurance reserve Binance created in 2018, funded by a portion of trading fees, used to compensate users after past security incidents.
