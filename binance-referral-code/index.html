What the welcome reward typically includes

Binance has historically structured its new-user reward in stages tied to specific actions: completing identity verification, making a first deposit above a minimum threshold, and placing a first trade within a set window (commonly two weeks). Each step unlocks a portion of the total reward, usually distributed as a trading fee rebate voucher rather than as free-standing cash. Exact amounts and thresholds change over time and by region, so treat any specific dollar figure you see online as a snapshot, not a permanent guarantee, and confirm the current offer on Binance's own registration or rewards page.

The lifetime fee discount

Separately from any one-time welcome bonus, signing up through a referral link typically applies an ongoing discount on trading fees that doesn't expire the way promotional vouchers do. This is usually the more valuable part for anyone who plans to keep trading over time, since it compounds with every trade rather than being a single use credit.

Is it safe to use a referral code?

Yes. A referral code is just a tracking identifier that tells Binance which account gets credit for the referral. It does not grant the referrer any access to your account, funds, or personal information. The only thing to verify is that you're signing up on the real binance.com domain rather than a lookalike site, since fake pages sometimes impersonate exchanges to steal login credentials.

Frequently asked questions

Can I add a referral code after I already have an account?

No. Referral codes can only be applied during the initial sign-up process, not added retroactively to an existing account.

Does using a referral code cost me anything extra?

No. It doesn't add any cost. If anything, it typically reduces your trading fees compared to signing up without one.

Is the referrer able to see my account or funds?

No. A referral code only links accounts for reward-tracking purposes. It does not give the referrer any access to your account.
