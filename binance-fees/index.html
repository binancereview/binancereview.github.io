Binance Fees Explained

Spot trading fees

Standard spot trading costs 0.1% per side, whether you're the maker (placing a limit order that sits on the book) or the taker (placing an order that fills immediately). Holding BNB and opting to pay fees with it gets you a 25% discount, bringing the effective rate down to 0.075%. On USDC pairs specifically, taker fees are slightly lower even before any discount.

Futures fees

Futures trading uses a different schedule: roughly 0.02% for makers and 0.05% for takers on standard contracts, with a BNB discount of up to 10% available on top. Note that futures and margin trading involve leverage and are restricted or unavailable in a number of countries, so availability depends heavily on where you're located.

VIP tiers

Binance lowers fees further as your 30-day trading volume and BNB holdings increase, through a VIP tier system running from VIP 0 up to VIP 9. Entry-level tiers require a moderate volume threshold, while the top tiers require very large monthly volume, well beyond what a casual user would reach. For most retail users, the realistic savings come from the BNB discount and referral code, not from VIP volume tiers.

Withdrawal fees

Withdrawal fees are charged per network, not as a flat percentage, and they shift with blockchain network conditions. A Bitcoin withdrawal, for example, typically costs a small fixed amount of BTC that adjusts based on network congestion. Choosing a cheaper network (where the same asset supports more than one) can meaningfully reduce this cost.

How the referral discount stacks

Signing up through a referral link typically applies an ongoing trading fee discount on top of whatever else you qualify for, and this discount does not expire the way promotional bonuses sometimes do. Combined with the BNB payment discount, the total reduction from the base 0.1% rate can be significant for an active trader.

Frequently asked questions

What is Binance's base spot trading fee?

0.1% per side for both maker and taker orders, before any discounts.

How much does paying with BNB save?

Up to 25% off spot trading fees and up to 10% off futures fees, subject to change with notice from Binance.

Are futures fees the same as spot fees?

No. Futures uses a separate, generally lower percentage schedule, but futures trading itself is restricted or unavailable in several countries.
